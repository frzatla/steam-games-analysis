**<h1> Steam Games Market Analysis </h1>**

**<h3> Project Overview </h3>**

This project analyzes the Steam Games dataset from Kaggle to identify what drives video game success on the platform. By exploring genres, pricing models, and release timing, the analysis uncovers how these specific factors impact player engagement and estimated sales as of December 2025.

**<h3> Key Objectives: </h3>**
- Identify correlations between Genre and User Ratings.
- Identify the optimal price range that maximizes sales for Indie vs. AAA titles.
- Determine the most optimal month to release a game.

**<h3> Data Source: </h3>**
Dataset is created by Martin Bustos on Kaggle. Data is collected from both the API provided by Steam themselves and Steam Spy, a Steam stats service based on Web API provided by Valve. <br>
Covers game up until the release year of 2025. <br>

**<h3> Conclusion </h3>**

User satisfaction on Steam favors complete, easy-to-play experiences, with Casual and Indie games holding the highest average ratings (0.77). Conversely, Massively Multiplayer (MMO) games are the lowest-rated genre (0.64), heavily penalized by players for bugs, server problems, and long-term content droughts. Annualized Sports and Racing franchises also suffer from low ratings, often review-bombed by players who feel the yearly releases lack meaningful updates. Furthermore, while the Action genre has the third-highest volume of releases, its lower average rating suggests the market is saturated with lower-quality titles that drag down overall player satisfaction.

Financially, the $30–$60 tier is the optimal price point for both Indie and AAA studios, generating the highest median owners and average base revenue. Even though the vast majority of Indie games are priced under $10, this strategy yields the lowest base revenue. Indie developers who price their games in the $30–$60 range earn nearly three times more on average than those charging $15–$30. Pushing the price above $60 causes a sharp drop in sales volume, showing that expensive deluxe editions rarely make up for the lost buyers. However, these base revenue metrics do not account for in-game purchases or DLCs, which often supplement the income of cheaper or free-to-play titles.

When it comes to release timing, average sales volume spikes from June through August, aligning closely with the Steam Summer Sale. Mathematically, August is the ideal month to launch a game. It provides the highest ratio of average owners per competitor, allowing new releases to capture high player interest while facing the lowest number of competing launches. Still, developers must weigh this historical advantage against real-time factors like major competitor release dates and current internet trends.
